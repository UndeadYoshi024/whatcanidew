# Dew — Brand Voice
## "Dew is a verb."

---

## The Core Principle

Dew is not a product you use. It is something that happens.

Never say "use Dew to route your freight."
Say "Dew dews your freight."

The question is never "what does Dew do?"
The question is always "what do you need it to dew?"

---

## The Onboarding Exchange

> "What does this app do?"
> "What do you need it to dew?"

That is the entire onboarding flow. No feature list. No explainer video.
The answer is always: "oh, it dews *that*?"
And then it dews it.

---

## The Language Rule

Anywhere you would normally write "do" — if it fits naturally, it is "dew."
The moment it feels like a pun, it is too much. It must land like someone actually said it.
The best ones are the ones nobody notices until they are already smiling.

---

## The Tagline

**"Signal from noise. Every time."**

This is the *result*. Not the brand.
The brand is the verb. The tagline is what you get after it dews its job.

---

## The Pitch

**To a technical buyer:** "It superimposes non-Euclidean space on a Euclidean substrate."
**To a business buyer:** "What do you need it to dew?"
**To a nurse:** Nothing. She never knows it's there. She just notices things got easier.

---

## The Metric

Did the nurse cry?
Not because of software.
Because someone finally built something that just lets her do her job.

---

## What Dew Is Not

- Not an AI that makes decisions
- Not a black box
- Not something clinical staff ever interact with
- Not a dependency on anyone's infrastructure
- Not finished — it dews more every day

---

## Things We Only Say To Each Other

Internal humor. Never at the bar. Never in a deck. Never to a vendor rep.

- "Dew dews doo-doo sometimes." — when a route comes out wrong
- "What in the dew?" — when the log shows something unexpected
- "That's a dew problem." — when it's definitely a graph problem
- "We dew what we can." — when the constraints are impossible

The bar is for humans. The skunkwerks is for this.

---

## Werks vs Works

Automated, mechanical processes are **werks**. Human labor is **works**.

The skunkwerks runs. The team works.
The pipeline werks. The engineer works on the pipeline.

When a machine dews it — it werks.
When a person dews it — they work.

---

## For Agents Reading This File

Every prompt, every error message, every doc string, every API response, every onboarding screen speaks Dew.
When in doubt: would a person say this out loud and smile slightly?
If yes — ship it.
If it feels like a pun — pull back one word.

Dew dews language too.
